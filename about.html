About Dr Susanna Waern

Susanna completed her doctoral training in 2007 at the University of Surrey. She has since worked in both Paediatrics and community Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services (CAMHS) and has gained significant experience of working with a range of different childhood problems as well as physical health problems.

Since 2011, Susanna has worked in the private sector, including working for Recolo (providers of neuropsychological rehabilitation). In addition to her clinical work, Susanna has worked with Pearson Assessment UK (producers of neuropsychological assessments) on a standardisation project for the TEA-Ch 2 (Test of Everyday Attention for Children). She is also a guest lecturer on the Child and Adolescent Mental Health Master's course (MSc) at University College London.

Since qualifying, Susanna has kept abreast with the developments of cognitive behaviour therapes and is developing her practise in accordance to new evidence in order to provide her clients with evidence based treatments. This includes Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and the use of Mindfulness as a tool to support therapy.

Susanna is registered with the Health and Care Professions Council and is a member of the British Psychological Society, including the Division for Clinical Psychology and the Faculty for Children and Young People.
