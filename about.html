About Dr Susanna Waern

Susanna completed her doctoral training in 2007 at the University of Surrey. She has since worked both in Paediatrics and community Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services (CAMHS). She completed her Postgraduate Diploma in Paediatric Neuropsychology at University College London in 2013. She currently works part-time in the NHS providing psychological and neuropsychological services to children with cancer, with a specific focus on children with brain tumours. Over the years, Susanna has gained significant experience of working with a range of different childhood problems as well as physical health problems.

In 2011, Susanna started working in the private sector, including working as an Associate for Recolo, a service providing neuropsychological rehabilitation in the community. In addition to her clinical work, Susanna has worked with Pearson Assessment UK (producers of neuropsychological assessments) on a standardisation project for a new neuropsychological assessment tool. She is also a guest lecturer at a number of universities, including University College London.

Susanna is committed to providing a evidence-based service and since qualifying, she has kept abreast with new research and developed new skills through further training in order to ensure that her practice is continuously developing. However, she is also highly conscious of the importance of the therapeutic relationship and her uttermost aim is to provide a child-friendly service, using a range of creative ideas to engage children and young people.

Susanna is registered with the Health and Care Professions Council and is a member of the British Psychological Society, including the Division for Clinical Psychology and the Faculty for Children and Young People.
